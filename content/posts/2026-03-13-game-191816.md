Starting this summer, all games featuring loot boxes will be rated a minimum of PEGI 16, a significant shift in how the gaming industry addresses the potential risks associated with in-game purchases. This change, announced by the European game ratings board, aims to protect younger audiences from the implications of paid random items that can lead to gambling-like behaviors.

The new regulations will classify games with loot boxes as PEGI 16 by default, restricting their sale to anyone 15 or younger across Europe. This move aligns PEGI's standards with those of other rating authorities, such as Germany's USK, which implemented similar measures recently. As a result, popular franchises like EA Sports FC may see their ratings increase, impacting how these games are marketed and sold.

As the gaming landscape evolves, it will be interesting to see how developers respond to these new regulations. Will they adapt their monetization strategies, or will they continue to rely on loot boxes despite the potential backlash? Only time will tell.
