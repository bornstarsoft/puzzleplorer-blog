Sony's recent State of Play presentation focused exclusively on games from Japan and Asia, delivering a treasure trove of 28 announcements in just 40 minutes. From the highly anticipated remake of Dragon Quest VII, set to release on February 5, 2026, to the nostalgic return of the Tokyo Xtreme Racer series, there's something for every gamer to look forward to.

Among the highlights, the reveal of Fatal Frame II: Crimson Butterfly Remake with its March 12, 2026 release date, and the exciting new gameplay trailer for Elden Ring's first major DLC, Nightreign: The Forsaken Hollows, set for December 4, 2025, have certainly caught the attention of fans. Additionally, the introduction of a new PS5 Digital Japanese Edition at a lower price point is a strategic move that could reshape the console's accessibility in the region.
