Fifteen years ago, Capcom released "Ghost Trick," a game that brilliantly fused narrative and gameplay, showcasing the potential of video games as a storytelling medium. Created by Shu Takumi, the mind behind the beloved "Ace Attorney" series, this inventive puzzle game invites players to step into the ethereal shoes of Sissel, a spirit on a mission to uncover the mystery of his own death while saving others from similar fates.

What sets "Ghost Trick" apart is its unique gameplay mechanics that allow players to possess and manipulate objects within the environment. This innovative approach not only enhances the puzzle-solving experience but also deepens the narrative, as each action taken by Sissel directly influences the unfolding story. The game is filled with quirky characters and a blend of humor and tragedy, making it a memorable experience that resonates even today.
